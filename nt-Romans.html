New Testament Commentary

The Book of Romans

Romans 1, 2, 3

During Paul's travels in the Book of Acts he wrote letters to the fledgling churches that were sprouting up around the Mediteranean. For fans of history, you will find it interesting that the "books" we have today in the New Testament are the actual letters Paul wrote to those churches - Galatians (written 49/50 AD), 1 Thessalonians (51 AD), 2 Thessalonians (52 AD), 1 Corinthians (55 AD), 2 Corinthians (55 AD), Romans (57 AD), Ephesians (60 AD), Colossians (60 AD), Philemon (60 AD), Philippians (61 AD), 1 Timothy (63-65 AD), Titus (63-65 AD), and 2 Timothy (67/68 AD).

The Book of Romans is the first of these letters that the New Testament throws at us after the Book of Acts. In chapter 1 Paul lays out the indictment against humanity showing how a world apart from God has and always will descend into choas and wrong thinking. Romans 1: "18 The wrath of God is being revealed from heaven against all the godlessness and wickedness of people, who suppress the truth by their wickedness, 19 since what may be known about God is plain to them, because God has made it plain to them...21 For although they knew God, they neither glorified him as God nor gave thanks to him, but their thinking became futile and their foolish hearts were darkened. 22 Although they claimed to be wise, they became fools..."

The beauty of Christianity is there are no "hidden" secrets. There is no "mystery" or "mystical" practices that lead to salvation and eternal life. God would be "unjust" if that were so. The same exact story that was told the day after Jesus was raised from the dead is the same exact story that we read in the Gospels 2000 years later. In direct contradiction to the Word of God, the Catholics by their papal decrees, Mormons by the teachings of Joseph Smith, or even the maniacs like the guys from Waco or Jonestown... all tell you that you need something "more than faith in Jesus" to be saved. They all claim to hold some "secret" message or "special" mandate to the path towards God. These claims are all blatant lies. Read the first 4 books of the New Testament (the "Gospels") for yourselves and compare the teachings of Jesus with the practices of these institutions and I am sure God will lead you to the correct conclusion.

Paul tells us in Romans 3 how to be a faithful Christian: "23 for all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God, 24 and all are justified freely by his grace through the redemption that came by Christ Jesus. 25 God presented Christ as a sacrifice of atonement, through the shedding of his blood—to be received by faith. He did this to demonstrate his righteousness, because in his forbearance he had left the sins committed beforehand unpunished— 26 he did it to demonstrate his righteousness at the present time, so as to be just and the one who justifies those who have faith in Jesus."

Romans 4, 5, 6

Faith vs. Works...an age old question. It was THE question of the Protestant Reformation causing, for the first time in over 1000 years, a break from the "Catholic" Church theology. The word "Catholic" is simply the latin word for "Universal". The Eastern Orthodox Church split off before the Protestants and claimed to be the true continuation of the original "Catholic" (Universal) Church. Either way, before the Protestant Reformation, all forms of the "Universal" Christian Church taught that man's "Works" produced salvation (e.g., one had to do good things and prove their worth through human endeavor in order to obtain eternal life with God).

But, most of you should know the story, in 1517 Martin Luther tacked some paperwork on a Catholic Church door letting them know how wrong they were and thereafter the Protestant Reformation was born. The Protestant Reformation's premise was that a person can only be saved by "Faith" and not "Works". Good "Works" were just a byproduct of a saving "Faith". Luther's theology (in my opinion) accords with the teachings of Christ in the Gospel. Paul would concur with Luther, as he states in Romans 4:

"2 If, in fact, Abraham was justified by works, he [would have] had something to boast about - but not before God. 3 What does the Scripture say? 'Abraham believed God, and it was credited to him as rigteousness.'... 13 It was not through law [e.g., Works] that Abraham and his offspring received the promise that he would be heir of the world, but through the righteousness that comes by faith... 23 The words 'it was credited to him' were written not for him alone, 24 but also for us, to whom God will credit righteousness - for us who believe in him who raised Jesus our Lord from the dead. 25 He was delivered over to death for our sins and was raised to life for our justification."

Therefore, Salvation is a GIFT from God and not something we can ever EARN through our own works in this life. Jesus came, as an innocent man and was killed for teaching the Truth. This death penalty was required because God is Holy and His Holiness requires the complete absence of sin for anyone to hope to stand in His presence one day. Man could never do enough to make himself Holy enough to stand before God at the end of our lifetimes. So, Jesus had to die and be raised from the dead, pay the penalty for the sinful nature that is within all of us, in order to cleanse us of all unrighteousness by our "faith" in that Truth. Here lies the simplicity of Christianity, namely, all we need to do is believe that Jesus died for our sins and was raised from the dead and such faith in Him will be credited to us in the end as righteousness. It is our faith and not our works that will allow us to stand before a Holy and Rigtheous God one day.

Romans 13, 14, 15, 16

Paul was unjustly beaten, imprisoned and ultimately murdered by the Roman authorities for nothing more than preaching the Gospel to others. But, he had a deep and abiding faith that God was in control of all things including civil government. Since God has ordained all government and has made all things, Paul praises the same government that persecuted him unto death, and shows us what our role should be in the order of things - namely, to obey authority and to love one another as Christ loves us. If we are mistreated or abused by the authorities of our day, I urge you to think of Paul's message here in Romans 13, and keep in mind this is the same authority that persecuted him everywhere he went. We must remember that "vengence" is not ours to dole out as Christians. We are called to pray and love our enemies in order to show a sinful world that there is a different way - God's Way - that brings light to the darkness. Thank you Lord for giving us a timely message this morning in Paul's words and in Your Wisdom on how to deal with the face of injustice in this dark, dark world.
